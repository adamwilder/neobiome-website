Project Scope

What this research explores

NeoBiome is an independent research initiative investigating the design, governance, and lived experience of intentional eco-communities. This study explores how people integrate regenerative land use, community structures, and emerging technologies to build sustainable, meaningful ways of living — and what those models might teach us about the future.

Areas of inquiry

  • Design principles and governance models of high-functioning eco-communities
  • How residents balance individual autonomy with collective decision-making
  • The role of emerging technologies in community infrastructure and land stewardship
  • Tensions between ecological values and economic sustainability
  • Regenerative agriculture, food systems, and their community contexts

Who I’d love to hear from

  • Residents or founders of intentional communities, ecovillages, or permaculture projects
  • Urban planners, architects, or designers working at community scale
  • Technologists building tools for distributed governance or sustainable living
  • Researchers at the intersection of ecology, sociology, or futures studies
  • Policy makers or advocates in land use, housing, or regenerative agriculture
  • Anyone actively exploring a more intentional relationship with land and community
